## Overview

Designed, conducted, and published a conjoint analysis survey to quantify the qualifications parents value most when selecting a piano teacher. Identified affordability as the top driver and demonstrated that professional certification is valued equivalently to 3–6 years of teaching experience.

---

## Problem

There was no empirical data quantifying how parents weigh piano teacher qualifications against one another, particularly the perceived value of formal certification relative to experience and price.

---

## Solution

- Designed a conjoint survey targeting parents of piano students
- Collected and analyzed attribute-level utility scores across qualifications, experience, and pricing
- Identified relative importance of each attribute to inform teacher positioning and marketing

---

## Key Findings

| Finding | Detail |
| :--- | :--- |
| Top Driver | Affordability |
| Certification Value | Equivalent to 3–6 years of teaching experience |
| Method | Conjoint Analysis Survey |
