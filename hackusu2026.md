## Overview

Designed and executed a conjoint analysis survey at the market research level to evaluate a new product line for Red Button Vintage Creamery. Identified a potential profit of over $3 per unit within the Logan college student segment and earned 1st place for the final project presentation.

---

## Problem

Red Button Vintage Creamery, a local confectionary in Logan Utah, is considering an expansion to its current product line, which currently includes ice cream, pie, candy, popcorn, and cookies. Their goal is to add one or more products that will increase overall market share by bringing in new customers, specifically from the college student segment.
What do college students prefer most? What provides the biggest potential for profit?

---

## Solution

- Designed a conjoint analysis survey targeting Logan college students using Sawtooth's Discover software. We built a CBC conjoint analysis survey to force students to make trade offs in their decision making, revealing what their true preferences were. 
- Our survey included 6 attributes: type of product, flavor, calories per serving, sugar (g) per serving, protein (g) per serving, and price of the product. Each attribute had multiple levels to adequatley capture the preferences of the specified market. At the close of the survey, we analyzed responses using Sawtooth's software and visualizations and created the ideal product combining the most preferred level for each attribute.
- Our final product was Protein cookies and cream ice cream. Sugar and calorie content was the least important attribute among them all, while price and type of product was the most important to the market.

## Result
- Comparing our new product to a protein-less cookies and cream ice cream at Red Button using a market simulator, we identified a viable price point generating $3+ profit per unit, accounting for the estimated cost of adding protein.
- We also compared our new product to an existing competitor product, Protien Pints. We found in a market simulation that our product was the most preferred in the market because of the price, while the competition was less preferable than not buying anything at all.
- We presented all these findings and business recommendations to judges, acting as representatives for Red Button. We placed 1st in our category out of 7 teams.
